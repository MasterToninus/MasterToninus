Ph. D. Course

An introduction to symplectic and multisymplectic geometry 20 hours (12+8) Prof. Mauro Spera (UCSC - Brescia) and Dr. Antonio Michele Miti (Max Planck Institut - Bonn)



Outline

Outline (tentative) I Symplectic geometry (M. Spera - 12 hours) Symplectic manifolds Momentum maps Marsden-Weinstein reduction Geometric quantization Examples and applications: Lie group representation theory, fluid mechanics and knot theory, quantum mechanics II Multisymplectic geometry (A.M. Miti - 8 hours) Multisymplectic manifolds L-infinity algebras and homotopy co-momentum maps Examples and perspectives



Overview

The present course - intended for a mixed audience of mathematicians and physicists - provides a gentle introduction to the the basic ideas and techniques of symplectic geometry and its multisymplectic counterpart, with a view to their multifaceted applications in mathematics and physics. It is thus naturally divided into two parts and geared towards recently established or perspective multisymplectic extensions and refinements of the corresponding symplectic notions.
